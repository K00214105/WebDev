What is VTEC

Vtec is a bacterium that causes diarrhoea and produces a toxin which can damage your bowel wall. E. coli 0157 is the best known strain. People generally become ill 2-4 days after being infected but it can take longer for others. Most cases occur in the summer and children under ten catch it more easily than other age groups.

What is HUS

Haemolytic Uraemic Syndrome (HUS) is a serious life threatening condition up to 5% of people who develop HUS may die. VTEC infection causes HUS in about 5-10% of cases, particularly children under five and the elderly. HUS destoys the red blood cells and the kindeys fail. This may happen about a week after the VTEC symtoms start.

Public Health Action

VTEC is a notifiable infection. This means the laboratory or your doctor must report all cases of VTEC to the Department of Public Health. They assess all cases and if you have a strain which produces toxins then we need to identify if there has been spread. A public health doctor can advise you on protective measures.
